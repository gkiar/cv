Child Mind Institute — Center for Data Analytics, Innovation, and Rigor

New York City, NY

Director, Research Scientist (Senior Scientist Track)

I lead a research center with interconnected teams focused on basic research with multimodal data, research and development for clinically-oriented products and data science applications, and tool engineering for both patient- and clinician-facing products. The expertise across our group relies on a foundation of software engineering and data science, with domain expertise ranging from neuroimaging, computational neuroscience and quantitative psychiatry, and applied machine learning. Across our work, my group produces tools and resources that support both fundamental neuroscience and mental health care through high performance computing, the application of computational statistics, and the incorporation of machine learning tools to overcome common but impactful challenges.

In this role, I develop and execute research programs, obtain funding from both governmental organizations and private foundations or partners, and communicate scholarly outputs to the community. The core of my research portfolio involves evaluating and improving the trustworthiness of tools and techniques used in mental health research to inform decision making surrounding robust applications of data collection and data processing in care directly.

Notable achievements include: i) obtaining and NIH R01 award on first submission at the age of 29, ii) building and managing a Center spanning basic research to product engineering and 15 individual contributors working on over 30 distinct projects and with a 7-figure annual budget, iii) designing and building the Multimodal Brain/Body Imaging Core at the Child Mind Institute (1x Project Manager, 6x PhD-level scientists, 6x Analysts) in 6 months, iv) publishing several first- and senior-authors works in high profile outlets.
